Spring
Liver & Gallbladder • Wood element • March — May
The liver is the body's master detoxifier — processing hormones, filtering blood, producing bile, and clearing everything the body no longer needs. Spring is when the liver's energy naturally rises. If it is congested — from parasite load, poor diet, hormone excess, or suppressed anger — the rising energy has nowhere to go. PMS worsens. Migraines arrive. Irritability spikes. The body is asking to clear.
Emotion of the season
Anger, frustration, resentment, and the energy of new beginnings blocked by old patterns
Parasite & seasonal connection
Spring is the primary parasite cleanse season in both TCM and Ayurveda. Many parasites — particularly liver flukes, Giardia, and roundworms — become more active as temperatures rise and their reproductive cycles peak. The liver and bile ducts are the primary sites of parasite residence, and spring's natural liver energy creates the ideal window for clearing.
Signs of parasitic load in spring
Unexplained fatigue that worsens after eatingIron deficiency and low ferritin despite supplementing — parasites consume ironB12 deficiency — tapeworms specifically deplete B12Skin issues: hives, eczema, rashes and unexplained itchingGrinding teeth at night (bruxism) — a classic parasite indicatorAnal itching especially at night — pinworm signatureSugar and carbohydrate cravings — parasites feed on glucoseBloating and gas that does not respond to dietary changesBrain fog and cognitive symptoms driven by gut-brain axis disruptionAnxiety and mood instability from neurological interferenceChronic sinus congestion and post-nasal dripUnexplained weight changes despite no dietary change
Emotional & energetic connection
In TCM the liver holds anger — not just explosive rage but resentment, the slow burn of swallowed boundaries, and the frustration of a life that keeps repeating old patterns. Parasites are, at an energetic level, something that takes from you without your consent — living off your resources, your vitality, your nutrients. The physical and emotional clearing of spring work together: as the liver releases what it has been holding biochemically, the emotions stored there begin to move too.
Spring protocol
  • Week 1-2 — Preparation: Remove parasite food sources. Eliminate sugar, alcohol, and refined carbohydrates. Increase bitter foods: rocket, dandelion, endive, chicory
  • Week 2-4 — Active cleanse: Black walnut hull, wormwood, and clove (the traditional anti-parasitic trinity). Take on an empty stomach morning and evening
  • Throughout — Biofilm disruption: Enzyme supplements (serrapeptase or nattokinase) on empty stomach to dissolve the protective biofilm parasites hide within
  • Throughout — Binders: Activated charcoal or bentonite clay 2 hours after anti-parasitic herbs to bind and escort die-off toxins out
  • Full moon protocol: Parasites are most active and vulnerable during the full moon when their reproductive cycle peaks. Intensify the protocol for 3 days around the full moon
  • Bowel support: Adequate fibre and magnesium to ensure daily elimination. Parasites must leave the body — constipation during cleanse recirculates toxins
Full moon protocol
Parasites are most active and reproductively vulnerable during the full moon. Intensify your anti-parasitic protocol for 3 days around each full moon throughout the cleanse season for maximum effectiveness.
Herbs & plant medicines
  • Black walnut hull — juglone compound is lethal to parasites and their eggs
  • Wormwood (artemisia) — the most evidence-based anti-parasitic herb. Also used medically for malaria
  • Clove — destroys parasite eggs which other herbs cannot penetrate
  • Oregano oil — broad-spectrum antimicrobial and anti-parasitic
  • Garlic — allicin compound disrupts parasite metabolism
  • Papaya seeds — papain enzyme destroys parasite protein structures
  • Pumpkin seeds — cucurbitacin paralyses parasites and facilitates elimination
  • Diatomaceous earth (food grade) — mechanical destruction of parasites through the digestive tract
Foods as medicine
  • Raw garlic — eat 2-3 raw cloves daily
  • Pumpkin seeds — handful daily, ideally ground
  • Papaya and papaya seeds — the seeds especially
  • Bitter greens: rocket, dandelion leaf, endive
  • Turmeric and ginger — anti-inflammatory support during die-off
  • Lemon water first thing — stimulates bile flow
  • Fermented foods — restore gut flora after clearing
  • Fibre-rich foods: ground flaxseed, psyllium husk
Nutrient restoration
  • Iron with vitamin C — replenish what parasites consumed. Test ferritin after cleanse
  • B12 sublingual — particularly if tapeworm was suspected
  • Zinc — parasites deplete zinc significantly. Essential for immune restoration
  • Magnesium — replace what was lost to both parasites and the cleanse process
  • Probiotics — high-dose multi-strain to restore the microbiome after clearing
Die-off support
Die-off (Herxheimer reaction) is the intensification of symptoms as parasites die and release toxins. This is normal and indicates the protocol is working. Support: increase water, use binders, take Epsom salt baths, rest more than usual, and reduce the dose if symptoms are severe.
“I release what has been living off my vitality. My body knows how to clear and restore itself.”
Summer
Heart & Small Intestine • Fire element • June — August
Summer is the season of the heart and small intestine in TCM. The small intestine governs absorption — discerning what is nourishing and what is not, at every level. This is the season for addressing intestinal parasites that disrupt absorption: Giardia, Blastocystis, and intestinal worms that steal nutrients directly from the small intestine before the body can use them.
Emotion of the season
Joy, connection, and the summer shadow: anxiety, overwhelm, and the inability to rest into abundance
Parasite & seasonal connection
Summer brings increased risk of water-borne and food-borne parasites — Giardia from water sources, Cryptosporidium, and various intestinal protozoa peak in warm months. This is also the season when the symptoms of intestinal parasitic load are most prominent because heat increases metabolic rate and parasite activity.
Signs of parasitic load in summer
Intermittent diarrhoea and loose stools — Giardia signatureBloating that is worse in heatNausea especially in the morningMalabsorption: eating well but remaining deficientHeat intolerance beyond what is expectedHeart palpitations — the small intestine-heart connection in TCMAnxiety that spikes in summerSleep disturbance — difficulty settling at nightExcessive sweatingFoul-smelling gas and stool
Emotional & energetic connection
The heart in TCM governs joy, connection, and the capacity to receive. When intestinal parasites disrupt absorption, the body literally cannot receive nourishment — no matter how well you eat. This creates a physiological state that mirrors emotional disconnection: present but not absorbing, there but not nourished. Summer's shadow emotion is the inability to rest into goodness — to receive the abundance that is available.
Summer protocol
  • Giardia-specific protocol: Berberine 500mg three times daily is as effective as metronidazole in studies for Giardia clearance
  • Avoid all unfiltered water and be cautious with raw produce
  • Digestive enzyme support — restore what parasites disrupted
  • Cooling anti-parasitic foods to respect the season's heat
  • Continue binder protocol: activated charcoal or clay
  • Reduce sugar intake completely — all intestinal parasites feed on simple sugars
  • Cold-pressed coconut oil — medium chain triglycerides are directly anti-parasitic
Herbs & plant medicines
  • Berberine — clinically proven against Giardia and intestinal protozoa
  • Goldenseal — berberine-containing herb, traditional anti-parasitic
  • Neem — Ayurvedic anti-parasitic, particularly for intestinal worms
  • Pomegranate peel — traditional and evidence-based for intestinal parasites
  • Coconut oil — lauric acid disrupts protozoan membranes
  • Ginger — anti-parasitic and digestive support
  • Calendula — soothes intestinal inflammation during cleanse
Foods as medicine
  • Raw coconut and coconut oil daily
  • Pomegranate — fruit and seeds
  • Cooling bitter foods: cucumber, mint, aloe vera
  • Pineapple — bromelain enzyme disrupts parasite structures
  • Fermented foods for microbiome support
  • Avoid ice cold drinks — they impair digestive fire needed for parasite clearance
Nutrient restoration
  • Fat-soluble vitamins A, D, E, K — Giardia and intestinal parasites cause fat malabsorption
  • Zinc — small intestine parasites deplete zinc severely
  • B vitamins complex — restore what malabsorption prevented the body from using
  • Digestive enzymes — restore proper absorption after clearing
  • L-glutamine — repair intestinal lining damaged by parasite activity
Die-off support
Summer die-off tends to manifest as increased heat in the body, skin flushing, and temporary intensification of digestive symptoms. Stay very well hydrated with mineralised water. Cooling foods and herbs support the process.
“I receive nourishment fully. What was taking from me is released and my body absorbs life completely.”
Autumn
Lung & Large Intestine • Metal element • September — November
Autumn is the season of the lungs and large intestine — the organs of intake and release. The large intestine is the final elimination pathway and the home of the gut microbiome. This is the second primary parasite cleanse season. As the body prepares to move inward for winter, clearing the large intestine of parasitic load creates the conditions for deep restoration.
Emotion of the season
Grief, letting go, and the emotion of holding on to what has already ended
Parasite & seasonal connection
Many parasitic organisms complete a reproductive cycle in autumn, making this the second most important cleanse window. Large intestine parasites — including various roundworms, hookworms, and pinworms — are best addressed in autumn. The lungs are also relevant: certain parasites including Ascaris migrate through the lungs during their life cycle, causing respiratory symptoms.
Signs of parasitic load in autumn
Chronic constipation or alternating bowel habitsPersistent respiratory symptoms: cough, post-nasal drip, bronchial issuesSkin conditions worsening in autumn: eczema, psoriasis, hivesRectal itching and discomfortLower abdominal cramping and discomfortUnexplained iron deficiency — hookworms consume blood directlyDifficulty letting go at every level: emotionally, physically, digestivelyGrief that feels bigger than circumstances warrantDry skin and mucous membranesImmune vulnerability as the season changes
Emotional & energetic connection
The large intestine holds attachment — the inability to release what has served its purpose. Parasites in the large intestine are literally preventing the body from letting go. The emotional and physical parallel is profound: autumn is the season of conscious release — of leaves falling, of what is complete being allowed to complete. A parasite-burdened large intestine cannot fully let go. The clearing is simultaneously physical and emotional.
Autumn protocol
  • Repeat the spring cleanse trinity: black walnut, wormwood, and clove
  • Add specific large intestine support: psyllium husk and bentonite clay flush
  • Castor oil pack over the abdomen 3 nights per week — supports lymphatic drainage and liver-large intestine connection
  • Enema or colonic consideration for significant load — facilitates physical removal
  • Full moon intensification protocol — same as spring
  • Address the lungs: oregano oil and thyme for respiratory parasite support
  • Grief work alongside the physical cleanse — they move together in autumn
Full moon protocol
Parasites are most active and reproductively vulnerable during the full moon. Intensify your anti-parasitic protocol for 3 days around each full moon throughout the cleanse season for maximum effectiveness.
Herbs & plant medicines
  • Black walnut hull and wormwood — the autumn repeat
  • Thyme — anti-parasitic and specifically supportive for respiratory involvement
  • Oregano oil — for respiratory and intestinal parasites
  • Slippery elm — soothes and restores the intestinal lining
  • Marshmallow root — lung and intestinal membrane support
  • Astragalus — immune support through the cleanse
  • Elderberry — immune strengthening for the seasonal transition
Foods as medicine
  • Pumpkin seeds — the classic autumn anti-parasitic food
  • Squash and pumpkin — warming and anti-parasitic
  • Garlic and onion — allium family at their seasonal best
  • Fermented foods: sauerkraut and kimchi — microbiome restoration
  • Root vegetables — grounding during the clearing
  • Warm spiced foods: cinnamon, ginger, cardamom
  • Reduce cold and raw foods — protect digestive fire
Nutrient restoration
  • Iron with vitamin C — hookworms cause direct blood loss requiring urgent iron restoration
  • Vitamin A — lung tissue repair. Particularly important in autumn
  • Zinc — immune and gut lining restoration
  • Probiotics — especially spore-based strains for large intestine restoration
  • Collagen and L-glutamine — intestinal lining repair after clearing
Die-off support
Autumn die-off often manifests as emotional intensity alongside physical symptoms — grief, sudden sadness, or old memories surfacing. This is the body releasing at multiple levels simultaneously. Honour both the physical and emotional aspects. Rest, warmth, and gentleness are essential.
“I release with ease. What has served its purpose leaves me now — physically, emotionally, completely.”
Winter
Kidney & Bladder • Water element • December — February
Winter is not a cleanse season — it is a restoration season. The kidneys govern the body's deepest reserves in TCM: the jing, the vital essence, the most fundamental life force. Winter is when the body needs to receive, not clear. After the spring and autumn parasite cleanses, winter is for rebuilding what was lost and strengthening the terrain so parasites have no hospitable environment to return to.
Emotion of the season
Fear, dormancy, and the deep rest that restores the body's most fundamental reserves
Parasite & seasonal connection
Winter is about terrain — making the body an inhospitable environment for future parasitic colonisation. A strong immune system, robust gut flora, adequate stomach acid, healthy bile production, and optimal nutrient status are all primary defences against parasites. Winter is when you build these. Parasite prevention is ultimately about the health of the host, not just the elimination of the parasite.
Signs of parasitic load in winter
Residual fatigue after autumn cleanse — indicates deeper restoration is neededCold extremities and poor circulation — kidney depletion signatureLower back and knee aches — kidney meridian in winterFrequent urination especially at nightReduced immunity and frequent illnessFear and existential anxiety — kidney's emotional expression in winterHair loss and premature greying — kidney essence depletionReduced libido through the winter months
Emotional & energetic connection
Winter holds fear — the deep existential fear of the kidney in TCM. After months of clearing, the body can feel vulnerable and exposed. Winter's work is building safety at the deepest level: nutritional safety, hormonal safety, nervous system safety. The fear that surfaces in winter is often old fear that the clearing has made accessible. This is not regression — it is the deeper layer becoming visible.
Winter protocol
  • No active parasite cleansing in winter — this is restoration season
  • Maintain a diet inhospitable to parasites: minimal sugar, adequate stomach acid, fermented foods daily
  • Build stomach acid: betaine HCl with meals supports the body's primary anti-parasitic defence
  • Prebiotic foods to build the microbiome that outcompetes parasites
  • Kidney-supportive herbs: nettle, horsetail, and rehmannia
  • Mineral-rich bone broth daily — rebuilds what the year has depleted
  • Adrenal restoration: ashwagandha and B vitamins through the winter months
Herbs & plant medicines
  • Ashwagandha — kidney yang restoration, adrenal rebuilding
  • Rehmannia — the primary TCM kidney tonic
  • Nettle — mineral-rich kidney support
  • Astragalus — immune building for the year ahead
  • Reishi mushroom — deep immune restoration and stress resilience
  • Schisandra — adaptogenic liver and kidney tonic
  • Maca — adrenal and hormonal restoration
Foods as medicine
  • Bone broth — mineral restoration and gut lining repair
  • Seaweed — iodine, selenium, and mineral density
  • Black foods in TCM nourish the kidneys: black beans, black sesame, blackberries
  • Warming root vegetables: sweet potato, parsnip, beetroot
  • Walnuts — kidney-shaped and kidney-nourishing in TCM
  • Slow-cooked warming stews — nourish at the deepest level
  • Mineral-rich water with sea salt
Nutrient restoration
  • Magnesium — rebuild the year's most depleted mineral
  • B5 and full B complex — adrenal recovery from the year's stress load
  • CoQ10 — mitochondrial restoration for sustained energy
  • Vitamin D — particularly important through the dark months
  • Iron — retest ferritin after autumn cleanse and restore to optimal
  • Iodine and selenium — thyroid and immune rebuilding
  • Zinc — immune and reproductive system restoration
Die-off support
There is no die-off in winter — only restoration. If you experience detox symptoms in winter, the body is telling you it is not yet ready for winter mode. Slow down the cleansing and increase the restoration.
